Water Pollution

  • Water pollution is a threat to the lives of water dwelling organisms and the ecosystems they support, food chains and habitats.
  • Pollution of water is as a result of several factors such as industrial effluent, agricultural effluent, sewage and plastics.
  • Contaminated water leads to various diseases such as stomach diseases, skin diseases, and contact with toxic substances.
  • There are measures that can be taken to minimize water pollution and they include; proper disposal of wastes, encouraging the use of environmentally friendly methods in farming and ensuring that laws are followed to the letter regarding water bodies.

Sources of Water Pollution

Pollution of water begins from different sources such as industrial, sewage discharge, agricultural drainage, effluent discharge, and plastic waste. Industrial effluent brings in undesirable substances and metals into water bodies, while agricultural runoff contains pesticides, fertilizers, and animal waste. Sewage disposal puts pathogens and biomass, and plastics add to the stock of litter. microplastics in aquatic environments.

Impact on Ecosystems

Water pollution has severe effects on ecosystems and the following are some of the effects that are observed. the mortality of water inhabitants, species diversity, and interference of food chains. Pollutants can lead to eutrophication, which is the excessive nutrients cause algal blooms that reduce oxygen concentrations, thus affecting fish and other water dwelling animals. The pollution of water also has an impact on the health of wetlands, rivers and lakes which are important habitats for many species.

Human Health Risks

People are affected by water pollution through direct consumption of impure water, touching water that is polluted, and drinking contaminated seafood. This can result in different health problems, such as gastrointestinal diseases, skin infections and chronic contact with hazardous materials such as heavy metals and chemicals, which may lead to severe health complications such as cancer and neurological diseases.

Economic Consequences

The economic effects of water pollution are severe. that impact sectors like fishing, tourism, and agriculture. The depletion of fish stocks and pollution of recreational water bodies may result in low earnings for the communities that rely on these resources. Also, the cost of purifying polluted water sources and solving health problems associated with water pollution is a cost to governments and individuals as a result of the disease.
